About QMET

Quantum Metrology Laboratory at Chungbuk National University — pioneering quantum sensing technologies and quantum computing algorithms.

Our Mission

We explore the full spectrum of quantum technology, from precision sensing that pushes measurement limits to quantum computing algorithms that harness the power of quantum mechanics for computational advantage.

Our laboratory serves as a bridge between fundamental quantum physics and real-world applications, training the next generation of researchers who can navigate both academic inquiry and industrial innovation.

Quantum Computing

We develop and implement quantum algorithms using two distinct paradigms: D-Wave quantum annealing systems for optimization problems and the IQM Spark gate-based quantum computer for universal quantum computation. This dual approach allows us to explore the strengths of each architecture.

Quantum Sensing Research

  • NV center-based magnetometry
  • Rydberg atom electric field sensing
  • Atomic magnetometer for brain signal measurement
  • Precision measurement beyond classical limits

Instrumentation Development

Beyond quantum research, we develop advanced instrumentation including 5-axis mirror alignment algorithms. This work cultivates versatile engineers equipped with precision optics and automation skills — preparing talent for both quantum technologies and broader industrial applications.

Inside the Lab

A glimpse into our research environment, equipment, and daily operations.

Quantum Sensing Lab

Our quantum sensing facility hosts NV center diamond setups, Rydberg atom chambers, and optically pumped magnetometer systems. Precision optical tables with vibration isolation provide the stability needed for detecting ultra-weak magnetic and electric fields.

Atomic Magnetometer Station

Dedicated to brain signal measurement research, this station houses alkali vapor cells, laser systems for optical pumping, and magnetically shielded environments. We develop OPM sensors for non-invasive magnetoencephalography applications.

Quantum Computing Access

We have access to both D-Wave quantum annealing systems and the IQM Spark gate-based quantum computer. Our researchers develop and test quantum algorithms remotely, bridging theoretical design with real hardware execution.

Precision Optics Workshop

Our optics workshop features 5-axis mirror alignment systems and custom automation setups. This facility trains students in precision instrumentation skills applicable to both quantum research and industrial optical systems.

Electronics & Control

Custom electronics development for quantum experiments, including photodetector circuits, RF signal processing, laser controllers, and data acquisition systems. We design the specialized hardware that enables our sensing and measurement research.

Daily Life

Research at QMET spans from algorithm development on quantum computers to hands-on sensor construction and characterization. Weekly group meetings foster collaboration across our diverse research areas, preparing students for careers in both academia and industry.

Our Research

Advancing quantum sensing technologies and quantum computing algorithms.

NV Center Magnetometry

Development of magnetic field sensors using nitrogen-vacancy centers in diamond. These solid-state quantum sensors enable high-resolution magnetometry at room temperature with nanoscale spatial resolution.

Rydberg Atom Electric Field Sensing

Exploring Rydberg atoms as ultrasensitive electric field sensors. Their extreme polarizability enables detection of low frequency fields with unprecedented sensitivity and broad frequency coverage.

Atomic Magnetometer for Brain Signals

Developing optically pumped magnetometers for magnetoencephalography (MEG). These sensors measure the tiny magnetic fields produced by neural activity, enabling non-invasive brain imaging without cryogenic cooling.

Quantum Annealing Algorithms

Implementing optimization algorithms on D-Wave quantum annealing systems. We explore applications in combinatorial optimization, machine learning, and materials simulation using quantum adiabatic computation. [reference: https://arim-ryou.github.io/]

Gate-Based Quantum Computing

Developing and testing quantum algorithms on the IQM Spark superconducting quantum computer. Our work spans variational algorithms, quantum error mitigation, and near-term quantum applications.

Precision Instrumentation

Engineering advanced research tools including 5-axis mirror alignment systems. This work develops automation and precision optics skills that bridge quantum research and industrial applications.
